    *Explanation here

The difference in results between the Bag of Words (BoW) model and the
TF-IDF model lies in the way they represent the features of the text
data.

\begin{itemize}
\item
  BoW simply counts the occurrence of each word in the document, whereas
  TF-IDF considers the importance of each word in the document relative
  to the entire corpus.
\item
  TF-IDF tends to give higher weights to words that are more unique to
  the document but less frequent in the corpus, which can result in
  better performance, especially when dealing with large and diverse
  datasets.
\end{itemize}

This is reflected in the potentially higher accuracy, precision, and
recall scores observed in the classification report for the TF-IDF model
compared to the BoW model.
